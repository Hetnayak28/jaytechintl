Metal Injection Molding

Advanced Manufacturing for Complex Metal Components

Overview

Metal Injection Molding (MIM) combines the versatility of plastic injection molding with powder metallurgy to produce complex metal parts with excellent mechanical properties. This process is ideal for high-volume production of small, intricate components with tight tolerances.

Key Features

  • Complex geometries possible
  • Excellent surface finish
  • High material density
  • Cost-effective for high volumes
  • Wide range of materials
  • Tight tolerances achievable

The Metal Injection Molding Process

1

Feedstock Preparation

Mixing metal powders with binder materials

2

Injection Molding

Injecting feedstock into precision mold cavity

3

Debinding

Removing binder material from molded parts

4

Sintering

High-temperature consolidation of metal particles

5

Heat Treatment

Optimizing material properties

6

Quality Control

Dimensional and property verification

7

Secondary Operations

Additional finishing if required
