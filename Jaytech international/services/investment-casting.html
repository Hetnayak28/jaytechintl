Investment Casting

Precision Metal Casting Solutions

Overview

Investment casting, also known as lost-wax casting, is one of the oldest and most precise metal-forming techniques. This process allows for the creation of complex shapes with excellent surface finish and dimensional accuracy that would be difficult or impossible with other casting methods.

Key Features

  • Excellent surface finish
  • High dimensional accuracy
  • Complex geometries possible
  • Wide range of alloys available
  • Cost-effective for small to medium production runs
  • Minimal machining required

The Investment Casting Process

1

Pattern Making

Creation of wax pattern by injecting wax into a metal die

2

Pattern Assembly

Multiple patterns are assembled onto a wax tree

3

Shell Building

Application of ceramic slurry and stucco to create a shell

4

Dewaxing

Removal of wax from the ceramic shell

5

Shell Firing

Heating the shell to achieve required strength

6

Pouring

Pouring molten metal into the preheated shell

7

Finishing

Shell removal, cutting, and final finishing operations

Applications

Aerospace

Turbine blades, structural components

Automotive

Engine components, turbocharger parts

Industrial

Pump components, valve bodies

Medical

Surgical instruments, implants
